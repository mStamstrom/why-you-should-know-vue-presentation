How VueJS updates the DOM

So, we just said that VueJS is performant because the main library is small and has built in optimisation. But how performant are the dom updates? First lets walk through how the update process works. Consider our previous example, at startup our app component will mount and thereby return a slice of virtual DOM. Virtual DOM? Why use a virtual DOM?

SO why use a virtual DOM, seems like a lot of work for just to update a 0 to a 1. First its an relatively easy approach, since we dont need to track data flow through our application. Also the virtual DOM thought is based on the assumption the components are cheap to rerender but expensive to update in the DOM. If we have components that are expensive to rerender, then that will slow down the whole update. I also dont think this is the part where VueJS shines. But spoiler, it will become better.
